About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto, based in Toronto, Canada, and advised by Prof. Andreas Moshovos. My research focuses on resource-efficient AI systems, including AI data compression, bit-level optimization, efficient neural network training, 3D graphics, and neuromorphic computing. I am also passionate about teaching and have served as a course instructor and teaching assistant for a variety of computer engineering and electronics courses. See my publications and news for recent updates.

Previously completed an M.Sc. (GPA 3.9) and B.Sc. (GPA 4.0, rank 2/260) in Electronics and Communications at Cairo University.

Research

AI Compression & Bit‑Level Optimization

Methods that reduce model and activation footprints while preserving accuracy, enabling larger effective capacity under tight memory and bandwidth limits.

  • Dynamic precision and containerization strategies
  • Hardware/Software co‑design for memory efficiency

Representative: Schrödinger's FP; Atalanta; Every Bit Matters

Efficient Neural Network Training

Training acceleration via selective workload skipping and adaptive resource allocation to reduce time‑to‑quality without compromising final accuracy.

  • Skip policies and curriculum‑style scheduling
  • Benchmarks and methodology for fair evaluation

Representative: Selective Workload Skipping (IHIET‑AI 2025)

3D Graphics & Gaussian Splatting

Computationally optimal pipelines for real‑time Gaussian splatting on mobile/AR devices, with attention to compression, latency, and visual fidelity.

  • Rate–distortion–latency trade‑offs
  • Scheduling and data layouts for fast rendering

Community: COGS (Computationally Optimal Gaussian Splatting)

Neuromorphic & Brain–Machine Interfaces

Scalable on‑device spike sorting and system design for untethered BMIs, emphasizing accuracy, energy, and robustness in deployment.

  • Throughput‑aware algorithmic pipelines
  • Energy‑efficient hardware/software interfaces

Representative: Marple (ASPLOS 2024)
